
# Chinese Numbers 1–99

## Summary

* Chinese numbers from **1 to 99** follow a highly regular decimal pattern.
* The key building blocks are **一 to 九** and **十**.
* **11–19** are formed as **十 + digit**.
* **20, 30, 40, …, 90** are formed as **digit + 十**.
* Other two-digit numbers are formed as **digit + 十 + digit**.
* For the numeral **2**, the standard form inside numbers is **二**, not **两**.

## Core pattern

The scheme is:

* **1–10**: basic numerals
* **11–19**: **十 + ones**
* **20, 30, …, 90**: **digit + 十**
* **21–99**: **tens digit + 十 + ones digit**

Canonical patterns:

* **十一** = 11
* **十五** = 15
* **二十** = 20
* **二十三** = 23
* **九十九** = 99

## Explanation

Chinese numbers from 1 to 99 are built transparently from the decimal structure.

### 1. Basic numerals

The digits are:

* **一** yī = 1
* **二** èr = 2
* **三** sān = 3
* **四** sì = 4
* **五** wǔ = 5
* **六** liù = 6
* **七** qī = 7
* **八** bā = 8
* **九** jiǔ = 9
* **十** shí = 10

### 2. Numbers from 11 to 19

These are formed with **十 + digit**.

* **十一** = 11
* **十二** = 12
* **十九** = 19

Chinese does **not** normally say **一十一** for 11 in standard Mandarin.

### 3. Multiples of ten

20, 30, 40, and so on are formed with **digit + 十**.

* **二十** = 20
* **三十** = 30
* **四十** = 40

### 4. Other two-digit numbers

Numbers such as 21, 36, 48, and 99 are formed with:

**tens digit + 十 + ones digit**

* **二十一** = 21
* **三十六** = 36
* **四十八** = 48
* **九十九** = 99

### 5. 二, not 两, inside numbers

When forming numbers themselves, Chinese uses **二**, not **两**.

* **二十** = 20
* **二十二** = 22

Using **两十** or **两十二** is not standard.

!!! warning
Inside numbers such as 12, 20, 22, or 92, use **二**, not **两**.

## Common errors / pitfalls

* Writing **一十一** for 11.
  Standard Mandarin uses **十一**.

* Using **两** inside number names.
  Use **二十**, **二十二**, **九十二**, not **两十**, **两十二**, **九十两**.

* Reversing 14 and 40.

  * **十四** = 14
  * **四十** = 40

* Forgetting the order of tens and ones.
  Chinese says the tens first:

  * **三十五** = 35
    literally “three ten five.”

## Comparison

### 1. 十四 vs 四十

* **十四** = 14
* **四十** = 40

This is one of the most important early contrasts.

### 2. 十二 vs 二十

* **十二** = 12
* **二十** = 20

The position of **十** determines the structure.

### 3. 二十二 vs 两个

* **二十二** = 22
* **两个** = two items

Inside numbers, use **二**. Before many measure words, use **两**.

## Notes

!!! tip
To build any number from 1 to 99, first identify the tens digit and the ones digit, then apply the regular pattern.

!!! note
For 11–19, Chinese starts directly with **十**, not **一十**.

!!! note
In careful learner notation, pinyin may be written either with or without separators such as apostrophes in forms like **shí’èr**. The purpose is only to make syllable boundaries easier to read.
